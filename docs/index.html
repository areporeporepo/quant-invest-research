Vinhomes Royal Island (Vũ Yên) (anchor 2026-01-01 at $3,875/m²)

  • bull (+14%/yr assumption) — 2027: $5,033 · 2028: $5,739 · 2029: $6,542 per m². If Thủy Nguyên truly becomes the administrative center of expanded Hải Phòng, Vũ Yên converges toward the Hanoi landed benchmark (~$7,100/m², CBRE Q4 2025). The new 2026 land-price table (~160 triệu/m² on central streets) resets the official floor beneath market prices; Lê Thánh Tông frontage across the river already asks 180-200 triệu/m². +14%/yr reaches ~$6,300/m² by end-2029 — still below today's Hanoi landed level.

Headline VND prices grind ~+8%/yr as the area matures — roughly Hanoi-condo-2025 pace — minus ~2%/yr VND depreciation gives ~+6%/yr in USD. That path takes ~$3,900/m² today to ~$4,900/m² by end-2029.
  • bear (-8%/yr assumption) — 2027: $3,281 · 2028: $3,018 · 2029: $2,777 per m². National landed segment is already cooling (CBRE Q4 2025: Hanoi landed -15% y/y, HCMC landed -28% y/y on fringe supply). Vũ Yên headline prices sit ~2x the Hải Phòng city average while sales vouchers and 0%-interest programs mean effective prices are below headline — if policies unwind into price cuts, headline follows. 877 ha of island supply plus Green Paradise competing for the same investor capital; VND slide (~1-2%/yr) further drags USD returns.
